Public Offer Agreement

Last updated: July 2026 · Public offer for the provision of educational services

1. General Provisions

This document constitutes a public offer (proposal) addressed to any legally capable individual (hereinafter the "Client") to enter into an agreement for the provision of English language teaching services on the terms set out below.

Acceptance of this offer — that is, full and unconditional agreement with its terms — is deemed to occur through any of the following actions by the Client:

  • Payment for a trial lesson, a single lesson, or a package of lessons;
  • Written confirmation of a booking (by email, messenger, or via the website form), followed by attendance at the lesson.

From the moment of acceptance, this offer is deemed to be an agreement concluded between the Client and the Provider.

2. The Provider

Provider: Ally Lingua (private tutor, sole individual)

Place of Business: Ireland, EU

Email: ally.lingua@gmail.com

Ally Lingua is the teaching practice of a private English language tutor, without any registered legal entity or sole trader status involved. Some lessons (in particular, certain group classes) may be delivered not by me personally, but by another qualified teacher engaged by me on a freelance/subcontract basis to work under the Ally Lingua brand. In this case, as the Provider, I remain fully responsible to the Client for the quality and organisation of those lessons, on the same basis as lessons I deliver personally.

3. Subject of the Agreement

The Provider undertakes to provide the Client with English language teaching services in one of the formats presented on the website: private lessons, group lessons for children, Business English, IELTS/TOEFL preparation, or another format agreed between the parties. The Client undertakes to pay for these services on the terms of this Agreement.

The specific programme content, schedule, course duration and cost are agreed individually at the time of booking and may be recorded in correspondence (email, messenger) — such correspondence forms an integral part of this Agreement.

4. Cost and Payment Terms

  • Prepayment: lessons are paid for on a 100% prepayment basis — for a single lesson or a package of lessons, depending on the format chosen. A lesson or package is considered booked only once payment has been received.
  • Payment method: payment is accepted by bank transfer (IBAN). Payment details are provided by the Provider individually once the programme has been agreed.
  • Currency: the payment currency depends on the Client's country and bank, and is agreed individually when payment details are issued.
  • Cost: current programme prices are listed on the website under "Programmes" and may be confirmed by the Provider at the time of booking. Any change in prices does not affect lessons already paid for.

5. Delivery of Lessons

Lessons are held online via video call (the platform is agreed individually) at dates and times agreed in advance between the parties. The Client undertakes to ensure the technical means to take part in the lesson (a device and internet connection).

Where lessons are provided to a minor, responsibility for the child's timely attendance rests with the parent or legal guardian who entered into this Agreement.

6. Cancellation, Rescheduling and Missed Lessons

6.1. Group Lessons

Group lessons run on a schedule set for the whole group. If a student misses a group lesson (for any reason, whether or not advance notice was given), payment for that lesson is not refunded or rescheduled, and the lesson itself is not repeated for that individual student. This is because the pace and programme of the group cannot be adjusted for the absence of a single participant. At the Provider's discretion, the lesson materials may be shared with the absent student for independent review.

6.2. Private Lessons

If the Client cancels a private lesson without notice (on the day of the lesson or after the agreed start time), payment for that lesson is not refunded and the lesson is not rescheduled. If the Client gives advance notice of cancellation, the Provider will, where possible, offer to reschedule the lesson to another date and time by mutual agreement.

6.3. Cancellation by the Provider

If a lesson is cancelled at the initiative of, or due to, the Provider (illness, force majeure, etc.), the Provider will offer the Client a rescheduled lesson on another date. If rescheduling is not possible for objective reasons, the corresponding amount may be refunded to the Client or credited toward future lessons, as agreed between the parties.

7. Refunds

  • The full amount is refunded if the paid lessons have not yet started and the Client cancels at least 48 hours before the first lesson.
  • Payment for lessons already delivered is not refunded.
  • Payment for missed lessons is not refunded, on the terms described in Section 6 of this Agreement.
  • Refund requests should be sent to ally.lingua@gmail.com and are reviewed within 14 days.

8. Rights and Obligations of the Parties

The Provider undertakes to:

  • Provide services to a high standard and within agreed timeframes;
  • Inform the Client promptly of any changes to the schedule;
  • Ensure the confidentiality of the Client's personal data in accordance with the Privacy Policy.

The Client undertakes to:

  • Pay for lessons promptly on the terms of this Agreement;
  • Attend lessons at the agreed time, or give notice of cancellation in accordance with Section 6;
  • Provide accurate information when booking.

9. Liability of the Parties

The Provider does not guarantee that the Client will achieve a specific result (for example, a particular level of proficiency or exam score) within a specific timeframe, since learning outcomes depend on regular attendance, independent study and the student's individual characteristics.

The parties are released from liability for failure to perform their obligations if this is caused by force majeure circumstances: technical failures, loss of power or internet connection, illness, or other circumstances beyond the parties' control.

If a lesson is delivered by a teacher engaged by me on a subcontract basis, I, as the Provider, remain responsible to the Client for the quality, organisation and compliance with the terms of this Agreement, in the same way as for lessons I deliver personally.

10. Personal Data

The processing of personal data provided by the Client during booking and throughout the course of study is carried out in accordance with the Privacy Policy, which forms an integral part of this Agreement. This also applies where a lesson is delivered by a teacher engaged on a subcontract basis: such a teacher only receives the minimum data necessary and is bound by a written confidentiality agreement with me.

11. Term and Amendment of the Offer

This offer is valid indefinitely and may be amended by the Provider unilaterally. The current version is always available on this page, and the update date is shown at the top of the document. Terms agreed and paid for by the Client before any amendments remain valid in their original form.

12. Dispute Resolution and Governing Law

The parties will seek to resolve disputes through negotiation. The Client may send a complaint to ally.lingua@gmail.com; a response will be provided within 14 days. If a dispute cannot be resolved through negotiation, it will be settled in accordance with the laws of Ireland, which govern this Agreement.

13. Provider Details and Contact

Provider: Ally Lingua (private tutor, sole individual)

Place of Business: Ireland, EU

Email: ally.lingua@gmail.com

Payment Method: bank transfer (IBAN), details provided individually
